What CitroTechnician is

30-second version

Every CitroTech sale becomes a multi-year relationship. Properties get an install, then a Year-1 inspection, then a Year-2 final. CitroTechnician is the system that knows what's due, who's contacting whom, who's driving where, and who signed off when. Salesforce stays the source of truth for sold deals; this tool runs everything that happens after.

This is not a CRM, not a customer portal, and not a partner system. Tuned specifically to CitroTech's product lifecycle.

The job lifecycle

7 stages · automatic + manual

Drag-and-drop on the Pipeline updates stages — except Completed, which is only reachable through the tech's signature + PDF flow (skipping that would orphan the maintenance chain).

Maintenance cycles · the 2-year agreement

how the chain works

cycleIndex = where this job sits in the agreement. cyclesPlanned = how many annual visits the contract covers (default 2). Both editable per job for one-offs and longer agreements.

Completing a job spawns its successor automatically unless it's the final cycle. The chain can be ended early by deferring with a reason (cancellation, sold property, customer churn).

Daily playbooks

do this · in this order

🖥️  Ops Manager · Monday morning

  1. Open /dashboard. Scan the four counters: Overdue, Due in 60, Scheduled, This week.
  2. Hit /tasks. Anything the engine surfaced overnight is here. Each row is "do an outreach." Mark complete after the call.
  3. Open /pipeline. Drag every Confirmed job to Scheduled as you book the date.
  4. Open /map?trip=1. Cluster nearby jobs into a single tech run. Click pins → "Schedule as trip."
  5. Log every call/email/text on the job's slide-over (Communication button) — that's the customer's paper trail.

🖥️  COO · weekly review

  1. Dashboard → Needs Attention. Anything red is overdue revenue. Shouldn't grow week to week.
  2. Pipeline → Year 2 (final) badges. Those are renewal conversations to start now.
  3. Settings → Salesforce. Last sync should be < 24 hrs old, status SUCCESS.
  4. Settings → Team. Review tech assignments and active flags.
  5. Hit "Run the maintenance engine now" if something feels stuck — safe, idempotent.

📱  Field Tech · on a job

The Complete button is grey until a customer signature is captured. That's the only gate. Everything else (photos, checklist) is recommended, not enforced — if a customer is in a hurry, the tech can still close out.

Screen tour

where things live

Dashboard

/dashboard

The home screen. Four numbers at the top. "Needs attention" list ranks the next 6 jobs by urgency. Tech workload shows who's loaded vs. free. Recent activity is the team-wide feed.

Who: everyone signs in here.

  CitroTechnician — Dashboard

Pipeline

/pipeline

The Kanban. Seven columns. Drag a card from Outreach → Confirmed after the customer says yes. Click a card to open the slide-over with phone numbers, notes, history.

Who: Ops Manager & COO can drag. Viewers can read.

  Pipeline · 23 jobs

Map · trip planner

/map

California pinned by status. Toggle Plan a trip, click pins to batch-select, then "Schedule as trip" assigns the tech and dates in one move. Saves the round-trip we'd otherwise drive twice.

Who: Ops Manager & COO can plan. Techs see read-only.

  Map · trip mode on · 4 selected

Calendar

/calendar

Week view, three swim-lanes (one per tech), seven day columns. Drag an unscheduled job from the right rail into a day cell to schedule it. Conflict badge if two land on the same day for the same tech.

Tasks

/tasks

The Ops Manager's inbox. Auto-generated by the maintenance engine at T-90, T-60, T-30, and overdue. Each task links to the job that triggered it. Mark complete when the call is done. Add ad-hoc tasks anytime.

Jobs & Properties & Customers

/jobs · /properties · /customers

The data tables. Search, filter, export to CSV. Open a row → slide-over with the full job detail.

Settings

/settings

Team roles, checklist templates per product (System / Spray), CSV import, Salesforce sync status, and the manual "Run engine now" button.

Things that happen automatically

  • At T-60 days, every Upcoming job auto-flips to Outreach and a task lands in the inbox.
  • At T-90 / T-60 / T-30, reminder tasks are generated for the Ops Manager.
  • When a tech taps the first checkbox, the job moves to In progress.
  • On Complete: PDF service report stored, signature locked, child cycle created (unless final year), parent moves to Completed.
  • Each night at 2am, the Salesforce sync pulls Closed Won opps and creates new Install/Year-0 jobs.
  • Each morning at 9am, the maintenance engine fires reminders.

First-day checklists

  • Open /pipeline & drag one card to a new column

Field Tech

  • Sign in on your phone — same Microsoft login
  • Add to home screen (it's a PWA)
  • Confirm you see today's jobs at /tech
  • Tap one to read the brief & access notes
  • Tap "Directions" & "Call customer" — both work

If something looks wrong — overdue counts spiking, a job stuck in a stage, a tech missing a job — open the job's slide-over and check the Activity log at the bottom. Every stage change, assignment, and reminder is recorded with a timestamp and the user who did it. There's almost always an answer in there before you need to dig into the database.
